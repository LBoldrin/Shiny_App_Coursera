Central limit theorem

  • The central limit theorem states that if \(X_1,X_2,...,X_n\) are a sequence of independent random variables each having the same distribution with mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\) and \(\bar{X}_n\) denotes their mean (i.e. mean of \(X_1,X_2,...,X_n\)), then the distribution of the standardized variable \(Z_n=\sqrt{n}(\bar{X}_n-\mu)/\sigma\) converges to the normal \(N(0,1)\) distribution for \(n\rightarrow \infty\).

  • On such basis, also with a skewed distribution, the central limit theorem says that the average \(\bar{X}_n\) will approach a normal distribution for sufficiently large \(n\).

  • Using the standardized variable \(Z_n\) and adopting different sample size \(n\), the distribution of the mean of a candidate skewed distribution (e.g. exponential distribution) is addressed in the following slides against the theoretical standard normal distribution \(N(0,1)\).

Exponential distribution

x <- seq(0,5,0.001);y <- as.data.frame(cbind(dexp(x,0.2),dexp(x,0.4),dexp(x,1)))
plot(x,y[,1],type="l",lwd=3,col="red",xlim=c(0,3),ylim=c(0,1.2),xlab="x",ylab="Density")
lines(x,y[,2],type="l",lwd=3,col="blue");lines(x,y[,3],type="l",lwd=3,col="green")
legend("topright",legend=c(paste("$\\lambda = 0.2$"),paste("$\\lambda = 0.4$"),
      paste("$\\lambda = 1$")),lty=c(1,1,1),lwd=c(1,1,1),col=c("red","blue","green"))

Monte-Carlo simulation

library(ggplot2);n <- 40;N <- 1500;lambda <- 1;x <- array(rexp(n*N,lambda),c(N,n));y <- 
as.data.frame(cbind(Stdz=sqrt(n)*(apply(x,1,mean)-1/lambda)/(1/lambda),Size=rep(n,N)))
p <- ggplot(y,aes(Stdz,fill=Size))+geom_density()+ylab("Density")+
stat_function(fun = dnorm, colour = "red",lwd=2)+xlab("Standardised mean")+
xlim(c(-4,4));p
